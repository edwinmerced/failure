Failure is an option

Failure is commonly known as lack of success, but any succesful entreprenuer will acknowledge the importance of failing. There are always postive lessons that can be learned from business ventures that have failed. Learning from one's failures, as well as the failures of others, can provide valuable insights for future success.

Three out of four start-ups fail (Gage, 2012). According to Patel (2015), 90% of start-ups fail. Failure percentage for start-ups is high; most start-ups fail (Henry, 2017). The reasons for start-up failures are multiple and varied. McCarthy (2017) identifies 20 reasons for start-up failures. Skok (n.d.) identifies 5 reasons for start-up failures.
